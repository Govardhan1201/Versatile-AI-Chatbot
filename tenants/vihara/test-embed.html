Spiti Valley: The Middle Land

A cold desert mountain valley located high in the Himalayas in the north-eastern part of the northern Indian state of Himachal Pradesh.

Mountain Budget Monastery

Why visit Spiti?

Spiti is one of the most rugged and remote regions in India. With an average altitude of 12,500 feet, it offers surreal landscapes, ancient Tibetan Buddhist monasteries, and star-filled night skies.

The name "Spiti" means "The Middle Land", i.e. the land between Tibet and India.

How to Reach

You can enter Spiti from two sides:

  • Via Manali: Cross the Rohtang Pass and Kunzum Pass. This route is open only in summer (June to October).
  • Via Shimla: Through the Kinnaur valley. This route is generally open year-round but is a longer drive.
