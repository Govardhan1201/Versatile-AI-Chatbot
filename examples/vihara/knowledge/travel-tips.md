# VIHARA Practical Travel Guide

## Planning Your Trip

### How Far in Advance Should You Plan?
- **Popular treks (Sandakphu, Dzukou):** Book permits and guides 2–4 weeks in advance during peak season.
- **Northeast India:** Minimum 3 weeks — ILP processing takes 7–10 days.
- **Spiti Valley:** 4–6 weeks for summer; 6–8 weeks for winter (logistics are complex).
- **Coorg and Majuli:** Can be done spontaneously, but homestays fill up during festivals.

### Budgeting
| Budget Level | Daily Cost (per person) |
|---|---|
| Backpacker | ₹800–1,500 |
| Mid-range | ₹2,000–5,000 |
| Comfortable | ₹5,000–12,000 |

Budget includes accommodation, local meals, transport within destination.

## Transport Tips

### Trains
Indian Railways is the backbone of offbeat travel. For Northeast India, catch trains to Guwahati or Dimapur. For Himachal/Uttarakhand destinations, take overnight trains to Delhi, Chandigarh, or Haridwar.

Recommended apps: **NTES**, **Rail Yatri**, **IRCTC** (for bookings). Book Tatkal tickets if last-minute travel needed (premium price, but seats guaranteed).

### Shared Jeeps and Tempos
The lifeline of hill and mountain travel. Sumo shared jeeps connect towns to villages across Himachal, Uttarakhand, Sikkim, and Northeast India. They depart when full — arrive at the jeep stand early.

### Rented Bikes and Cycles
Royal Enfield rentals available in Manali, Leh, Darjeeling, and Coorg. For Majuli Island and Coorg, cycling is the ideal — and most atmospheric — way to explore.

## Documents You Must Carry

### For All Travel
- Government photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport) — mandatory for hotel check-ins
- 4–6 passport-size photographs (required for permits)
- Downloaded copies of all permits on your phone
- Emergency contact list in paper form

### Inner Line Permits (ILP) — Northeast India
Required for entry to:
- **Arunachal Pradesh** — apply online at arunachalilp.com or from Arunachal Pradesh offices
- **Nagaland** — apply at nagalandeservices.nic.in (for non-Nagaland residents)
- **Manipur** — apply from Manipur House, New Delhi or district offices
- **Mizoram** — apply from Mizoram House offices

Cost: ₹100–500 depending on state. Processing: 1–7 days (online is faster). Validity: 7–30 days typically; can be extended.

### Protected Area Permits (PAP)
Required for:
- **Sikkim restricted areas** (Gurudongmar, North Sikkim) — apply via Sikkim Tourism Dept
- **Andaman & Nicobar restricted islands** — apply from Port Blair permit office

## Safety on the Road

### Altitude Sickness (AMS)
Symptoms: Headache, nausea, dizziness, insomnia, breathlessness. Prevention: Ascend slowly (no more than 500m gain per day above 3,000m). Stay hydrated. Do not drink alcohol in the first 2 days. Treatment: Descend immediately if symptoms are severe. Medication: Diamox (acetazolamide) — consult your doctor before travel.

### Mountain Road Safety
- Do not drive mountain passes in the dark
- Follow jeep drivers — they know road conditions
- Check BRO (Border Roads Organisation) road bulletins before Spiti and Ladakh travel
- Carry a spare tyre and basic car toolkit for long drives

### Mobile Connectivity
BSNL has the widest coverage in remote areas including border regions. Jio works in most tourist towns but fails in remote valleys. In Spiti, Dzukou, and parts of Northeast, there may be no connectivity at all — download offline maps before leaving.

## Visa and Foreign National Guidelines

Foreign nationals require Restricted Area Permits (RAP) for many destinations near borders. These are separate from and in addition to the standard Indian tourist visa. Areas requiring RAP include:
- Inner Line areas in Northeast India
- Most of Sikkim beyond Gangtok
- Spiti Valley (some checkposts check documents)
- Lakshadweep islands

Foreign nationals should check current regulations from their country's Indian Embassy and the Indian Bureau of Immigration before travel. Regulations change. VIHARA publishes updated guidelines for foreign travellers on each destination page.

## Food and Water

### Water Safety
Never drink tap water in India. **Always carry:**
- 2-litre reusable water bottle
- Water purification tablets (Iodine or Chlorine-based) OR
- UV purifier (SteriPen) — reliable for mountain streams

### Altitude and Food
At high altitude, digestion slows. Eat light meals. Avoid heavy fats and proteins in the first 2 days above 3,500m. Warm soups, khichdi, and dal are ideal. Avoid raw vegetables from unfamiliar sources.

### Local Food You Must Try
- Assam: **Masor Tenga** (sour fish curry), **Kharoli** (mustard paste chutney)
- Nagaland: **Smoked pork with bamboo shoot**, **Zutho** (rice beer)
- Himachal: **Siddu** (stuffed bread), **Dham** (traditional Pahari meal on leaf plate)
- Coorg: **Pandi curry** (pork with Kachampuli vinegar), **Akki rotti**
- Sikkim: **Sha Phaley** (fried bread with meat), **Chhurpi** (dried yak cheese), **Tongba** (millet beer)
- Majuli/Assam: **Apong** (rice beer), fresh Brahmaputra river fish curries

## Responsible Travel Checklist

- [ ] Research and follow Leave No Trace principles for your destination
- [ ] Hire local guides through verified platforms (not random touts)
- [ ] Do not photograph tribal communities or religious ceremonies without permission
- [ ] Carry your plastic waste out — no bins in many remote areas
- [ ] Pay local prices and be fair — do not haggle aggressively with small vendors
- [ ] Dress modestly when visiting monasteries and village religious spaces
- [ ] Don't pick wildflowers or disturb wildlife — especially in national parks
- [ ] Support homestays over hotels — money goes directly to local families
- [ ] Learn 5 words in the local language — it goes a long way
